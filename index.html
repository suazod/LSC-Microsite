Lone Star College is proud to announce that construction has begun on its new LSC-Airship campus

Planned for launch on April 1st, 2021

Population growth on the north side of Houston over the last decade has led to a shortage of real estate capable of supporting a full service campus, while leaving a large number of potential students without a place to attend classes. When trying to find a suitable place to build our eighth campus, we simply had to look up.

LSC-Airship will use an innovative combination of lighter-than-air technology with fixed and rotary wings. This will allow the campus to easily move about the Houston area, while never straying too far from its home base at George Bush Intercontinental Airport (IAH).

In the event of hurricanes or other natural disasters, this self-sufficient campus can simply take to the skies and wait for the danger to pass.

Need for an Eighth Campus

In the past four years alone, the LSC service area has grown by 176,000 people, more people than 39 US states added during the same time period. An additional 242,000 people are expected to increase the regions population to 2.55 million by 2021. The workforce in the LSC area has grown by 9% in the past five years. From fall 2015 fall 2020 LSC added more than 28,000 credit students a 57% increase.

LSC is the choice of area high school graduates 5,848 high school graduates attended LSC in fall 2020, 10 times the number of students choosing the University of Houston; six times as many who attended Texas A&M University.

Featured Programs

Along with offering standard two-year degrees such as Associate of Arts (AA) and Associate of Science (AS), Lone Star College leadership was committed to providing cutting edge information technology coursework to Houstons next generation of leaders. LSC-Airships flagship programs are the culmination of a long-standing dream to make cloud computing accessible to anyone in the city of Houston.

When asked why an IT program needed to be in the sky, Chancellor Lonny Starr replied, where better to learn about cloud-based computing than in the clouds? I think its a perfect metaphor for what we are trying to get across to our students.

To make this dream a reality, Lone Star College has partnered with Cisco Systems and Microsoft to develop new curriculum and degree plans including:

  • Cloud Networking AAS Degree, Cisco Track
  • Cloud Networking AAS Degree, Microsoft Track
  • Cloud Network Administrator Certificate: Cisco Track
  • Cloud Network Administrator Certificate: Microsoft Track
  • CCCNA (Cisco Certified Cloud Networking Associate)

LSC-Airship will also offer cutting edge curriculum in green energy technologies. A co-operative program has been set up with the airships crew that will allow students to get hands on experience working with the mechanics and electrical systems that keep LSC-Airship afloat literally.

Students will learn to maintain the actual solar panels, wind turbines, and fuel cells that power the campus. Degrees offered will include:

  • Fuel Cell Systems Electrician Marketable Skills Award
  • Solar Photovoltaic System Electrician Marketable Skills Award
  • Wind Turbine System Electrician Marketable Skills Award
